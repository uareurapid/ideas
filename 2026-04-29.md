## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: ElderSync**
Most families with aging parents struggle to coordinate across siblings — who took mom to the doctor, what medications she's on, when the next appointment is. ElderSync is a shared family dashboard for eldercare coordination: medical appointments, medication logs, caregiver shifts, and notes. Think CoParentSync but for the adult children managing aging parents.

**Idea 2: ShiftSwap**
Shift workers (nurses, retail staff, hospitality) constantly need to swap, give away, or pick up extra shifts — but internal systems are terrible group chats or paper boards. ShiftSwap is a marketplace within a workplace or industry where workers post and claim shifts with built-in approval from managers. Think Airbnb for work shifts.

**Idea 3: WarrantyVault**
When an appliance breaks, finding the receipt, warranty card, or purchase proof is a nightmare. WarrantyVault lets you scan receipts at purchase and auto-registers products with their warranty dates — sending reminders before they expire and storing everything in one place. Turns a painfully reactive process into a proactive one.

**Idea 4: DebtDiary**
Expense-splitting apps handle shared bills, but they don't handle the casual "I'll get you back" debts between friends that quietly accumulate. DebtDiary tracks informal IOUs between friends with a soft reminder system — no awkward confrontations, just a gentle nudge at the right time. Less formal than Splitwise, more like a shared ledger between trusted people.

**Idea 5: EventHost**
People who host recurring social events (dinner parties, game nights, book clubs) have no good tool to manage them. EventHost tracks who attended each time, what was served or played last session, suggests fair rotation for hosts, and handles invites. A lightweight CRM for your social life as a host.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: ExtensionShip 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
The Chrome Web Store has 3B+ users and most category niches have weak or abandoned competition — yet almost no indie devs build extensions as a distribution channel. ExtensionShip is a toolkit for converting existing SaaS web apps into Chrome extensions: boilerplate, toolbar UI components, Store listing builder with keyword optimization, and update pipeline scripts. One channel most founders skip entirely.

**Idea 7: ContextCarry 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers using multiple AI tools (Claude, Cursor, ChatGPT, Copilot) waste significant time re-explaining their project's stack, conventions, and context at the start of every session. ContextCarry syncs a structured project context file (codebase summary, tech stack, naming conventions, current goals) across AI tools via a lightweight browser extension or CLI hook. Write once, every AI assistant knows your project.

**Idea 8: LeadAudit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers and dev agencies struggle to find warm leads without manual prospecting. LeadAudit automatically scans a list of prospect websites for specific technical gaps — slow load times, missing error tracking, broken SEO metadata, no analytics — and drafts a personalized cold outreach email for each, citing their actual specific issues. Turns a cold list into warm, evidence-based pitches.
