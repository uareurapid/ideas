## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: FluentFade**
Language learners lose conversation skills within weeks of stopping practice — and nobody measures or predicts the decay. FluentFade tracks your vocabulary and grammar retention over time, calculates your personal forgetting curve, predicts when you'll drop below usable fluency, and schedules optimal micro-review sessions to prevent skill loss. Unlike Duolingo's daily streak obsession, this is about long-term retention across months of inactivity — the tool every language learner needs between trips abroad or after finishing a course.

**Idea 2: CarpoolRelay**
School carpools are organized through chaos — WhatsApp threads, sticky notes, and last-minute panic texts when someone's sick. CarpoolRelay builds a weekly rotation with fixed pickup/dropoff days per household, auto-generates the schedule, and handles swap requests with one tap when plans change. Notifies the whole group instantly when a swap is claimed, tracks who drove last, and keeps a shared calendar. Millions of parents coordinate school runs daily with zero purpose-built software.

**Idea 3: CurfewCheck**
Parents want to know their teen got home on time — but Life360-style continuous tracking feels invasive and breeds resentment. CurfewCheck is a simple check-in app where teens confirm arrival with a one-tap geotagged ping (no ongoing location sharing), and parents get notified only when a curfew deadline is missed. Balances teen autonomy with parent peace of mind — the middle ground that existing family trackers completely miss, and the one both sides would actually agree to use.

**Idea 4: SoundID**
You hear a great song in a bar, shop, or someone's car — but by the time you open Shazam, it's gone. SoundID lets you hum or whistle the fragment while it's fresh in your head, stores the audio snippet, and later matches it against music databases when AI can identify it. Keeps a "heard but unknown" diary of songs you're hunting with timestamps and locations. Shazam for the moments Shazam isn't fast enough — capture now, identify later.

**Idea 5: ScamScript**
Elderly people freeze during scam calls because they lack a practiced response — not because they can't spot the scam, but because they panic in the moment. ScamScript activates when an unknown number calls, displays a calm script on-screen ("I don't give information over the phone — who should I call back to verify?"), flags common scam patterns in real-time during the call using on-device audio analysis, and auto-reports suspicious numbers to designated family members. Doesn't block calls (which elderly people hate), but coaches through them.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: LandingRoast 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers spend hours tweaking their landing page with zero external feedback — asking friends gets polite lies, and posting to Reddit gets a chaotic pile-on. LandingRoast is a structured peer review platform where indie devs submit their landing page and get categorized, actionable feedback from other founders (copy clarity, visual hierarchy, trust signals, CTA placement) — earned by reviewing others' pages first. The "roast my landing page" subreddit as a purpose-built product, with reviewer reputation scores that incentivize quality over trolling.

**Idea 7: OnCallKit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Every growing SaaS hits the point where someone needs to wake up when the server dies — but setting up PagerDuty is absurdly complex and expensive for teams of 1–10. OnCallKit is a lightweight on-call rotation tool with a dead-simple schedule builder, multi-channel alerts (SMS, WhatsApp, Telegram, not just push notifications), configurable escalation rules for tiny teams, and a shared incident log. The on-call tool that doesn't require a DevOps engineer to configure — one YAML file and a webhook.

**Idea 8: PermissionAudit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Mobile developers get App Store rejections and Google Play flags because their app requests unnecessary or deprecated permissions — and justifying each one to reviewers is tedious guesswork. PermissionAudit scans your AndroidManifest.xml and Info.plist, cross-references against actual code usage, flags unused or overbroad permission declarations, suggests the minimum viable permission set, and auto-generates privacy justification text for each one. Prevents the most common App Store rejection reason before you even submit.

**Idea 9: DumpsterScore 📱 MOBILE APP**
The dumpster diving and freegan community is passionate, growing, and coordinated entirely through scattered Reddit threads and Facebook groups — zero dedicated software exists. **Idea 10: GoblinMode 📱 MOBILE APP**

Think Duolingo, but for becoming delightfully feral.
