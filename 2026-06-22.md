## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: RecallRadar**
People buy hundreds of products a year but never hear about recalls until it's too late. RecallRadar lets you scan or forward receipts, then cross-references everything against FDA, CPSC, and NHTSA recall databases — alerting you when that blender, car seat, or dog food you bought gets flagged.

**Idea 2: SharedLaundry**
Apartment building laundry rooms are chaos — you walk down three flights with a full basket only to find every machine taken. A simple booking system with machine status sensors (or manual check-in/check-out), time slot reservations, and SMS alerts when your cycle finishes or a machine frees up.

**Idea 3: FuneralPotluck**
When someone dies, communities rally with food — but coordinating who brings what and when is always a mess of text threads and duplicate casseroles. A meal train scheduler specifically for bereavement, with dietary preference collection, delivery time slots, and the option to contribute restaurant gift cards instead of cooking.

**Idea 4: DisasterPrepCheck**
Generic disaster checklists don't account for your actual location and season. This app tailors preparedness plans to your specific address — wildfire season in California, hurricane prep in Florida, earthquake kits in Tokyo — with inventory tracking, expiration alerts on stored supplies, and real-time evacuation route updates during active events.

**Idea 5: LanguageExchange Local**
Language apps connect you with partners across the world, but practicing in person is far more effective. This matches you with nearby language learners who speak the language you want to learn and want to learn the language you speak — coffee meetup optional, safety verification built in, with conversation prompt cards for structured sessions.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: RateLimitSimulator 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers discover rate limit bugs in production because there's no way to simulate them locally. This proxy sits between your dev environment and any third-party API, letting you inject realistic rate limit scenarios (429s, retry-after headers, quota exhaustion, burst throttling) so you can build proper retry and backoff logic before customers ever notice.

**Idea 7: BoilerplateBurner 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Projects accumulate dead weight — commented-out config blocks, unused environment variables, zombie CI pipeline steps, dependencies nobody remembers adding. This CLI scans your entire repo (code, CI configs, Docker files, .env templates) and flags everything that's likely dead or vestigial, with confidence scores and cleanup suggestions.

**Idea 8: DarkLaunch 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Feature flags let you toggle features, but orchestrating a gradual production rollout is still manual and nerve-wracking. DarkLaunch provides a workflow engine: ship code dark → enable for internal team → expand to beta group → 5% → 25% → 100%, with automatic rollback if error rates, latency, or crash metrics breach thresholds at any stage. Integrates with LaunchDarkly/Flagsmith + Datadog/Sentry.
