## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: SplitTab**
Splitting restaurant bills is still painful — most apps require everyone to download something or manually type amounts. SplitTab lets one person scan the receipt with their phone, automatically parses line items via OCR, and lets friends claim their items via a shared link (no app install needed). Settlement via Apple Pay / Revolut / PayPal in one tap.

**Idea 2: NeighborAlert**
Local community groups on Facebook and WhatsApp are chaotic and full of noise. NeighborAlert is a hyper-local push notification app (street/block level) for things that actually matter: power outages, road closures, lost pets, suspicious activity. No social feed, no comments — just relevant, geofenced alerts you opt into.

**Idea 3: MealMemory**
People forget what they cooked, what worked, and what their household actually likes. MealMemory is a simple meal journal — log what you made, rate it, attach a photo, and tag who liked it. Over time it suggests "best hits" when you're stuck for ideas and builds a personalized recipe box without having to save from cooking sites.

**Idea 4: ReturnRadar**
Online shoppers lose track of return windows constantly and miss deadlines. ReturnRadar scans your email inbox for order confirmations, extracts return deadlines automatically, and sends you a push reminder 3 days before each window closes. No manual entry, works across all retailers.

**Idea 5: CarLog**
Most people have no idea what they've spent on their car beyond fuel. CarLog is a dead-simple mobile app to log fuel, maintenance, repairs, and insurance — with a running lifetime cost-per-km and a reminder system for scheduled services. Targets car owners who don't want a full-blown fleet management tool, just clarity.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: LaunchWall 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers struggle to collect social proof early — they have happy users but no system to capture testimonials at the right moment. LaunchWall is an embeddable widget + SDK (React/Swift/Kotlin) that triggers a testimonial prompt at key in-app moments (after a user completes a task, hits a milestone, or upgrades). It formats responses as tweetable quotes, G2-style reviews, or structured JSON for import into your landing page — all from one integration.

**Idea 7: ColdDeck 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers building B2B SaaS know they need to do outbound but hate writing cold emails. ColdDeck pulls your product's positioning from your landing page and GitHub README, then generates a library of personalized cold email sequences targeting specific ICP segments (e.g. "CTOs at 10–50 person fintech startups"). Sequences are editable, A/B testable, and sync directly to Instantly/Apollo/Lemlist — no copywriter needed to get started.

**Idea 8: AppStoreGap 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Finding validated App Store niches is time-consuming — developers manually browse charts and reviews hoping to spot opportunities. AppStoreGap continuously monitors App Store and Google Play reviews across categories, surfaces recurring complaints in top apps, and shows keyword gaps where search volume exists but quality apps don't. Delivers a weekly digest of "validated gaps" with estimated revenue and competition scores, so developers can validate ideas before building.
