## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: SigningTable**
When someone is simultaneously buying a house, renewing a lease, signing a freelance contract, and taking out a loan, they lose track of which documents are pending their signature, which have been returned, and which counterparties need chasing. SigningTable is a receiver-side contract status dashboard: add any document you're waiting on or need to sign, track its status, set a nudge reminder, and log the signed version when done. Every existing e-signature tool is built for the sender — nobody has built the simple organizer for the person on the receiving end of six concurrent contracts.

**Idea 2: ShiftBid**
Hospitality, healthcare, and retail teams swap shifts through a chaos of WhatsApp messages where half the team misses the thread and managers end up fielding calls at 6am. ShiftBid is a lightweight shift exchange board for a single team: workers post shifts they need to offload, others claim them, and the swap is confirmed with one tap — with a manager-approval gate if required. Not a full HR or scheduling platform; just the "who wants my Thursday?" problem, solved cleanly.

**Idea 3: VisaCalc**
Digital nomads and expats living across Schengen countries manually count entry and exit dates on a calendar app, constantly anxious about overstaying their 90/180 rule. VisaCalc lets you log passport stamps as you cross borders, auto-calculates your remaining Schengen days, shows a "safe stay until" date for any given country, and fires a warning 2 weeks before you'd overstay. Covers Schengen, UK, US B-2, and other common nomad visa regimes — the one app that knows exactly how many days you have left before things get expensive.

**Idea 4: SpeakEasyBar**
Home bartenders accumulate a sprawling bottle collection but can never remember what they can actually make right now without a last-minute dash to the shops. SpeakEasyBar is a cocktail discovery tool built around your existing inventory: scan or enter the bottles you own, instantly see every recipe you can make right now vs. "one ingredient away," and browse curated variations that use up bottles you've had open too long. The Supercook for spirits — except it also teaches you the technique.

**Idea 5: SupperClub**
People who regularly host dinner parties have a recurring problem: they can't remember which dishes they served which guests, who's vegetarian, or whose partner hates fish — so they either ask awkward clarifying questions every time or accidentally serve the same meal twice to the same friends. SupperClub is a host's social dining log: record events, guest lists, dishes served, dietary notes, and short ratings, so next time you have the Martins over you know exactly what worked last time and what to avoid. The black book every habitual entertainer wishes existed.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: TechDebtTag 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
TODO, FIXME, HACK, and DEBT comments accumulate in every codebase — developers leave them intending to come back, then never do because there's no way to surface, prioritise, or track them. TechDebtTag connects to your GitHub repo, indexes every comment of that type, lets you assign priority and owner directly from a dashboard, and generates a weekly burndown chart showing whether your technical debt is growing or shrinking. Unlike full project management tools, it requires no ticket creation — the comment in the code *is* the record, and the dashboard just makes it visible and actionable.

**Idea 7: PaywallTestKit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers launching a paid tier have strong opinions about pricing but no structured way to test whether monthly vs. annual, 7-day vs. 14-day trial, or hard gating vs. soft nudging actually converts better for their specific audience. PaywallTestKit is a no-code A/B testing wrapper for your paywall: define variants (pricing options, trial length, feature gating copy), embed one SDK call, and get a clean conversion funnel comparison — without touching your billing provider integration or redeploying your paywall logic for each experiment. The "Optimizely for the checkout page" that takes an afternoon to add, not a sprint.

**Idea 8: HealthPageKit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Every SaaS app eventually needs a status page, but setting up StatusPage or Instatus requires configuring monitors, DNS, and a separate account most indie devs never get around to. HealthPageKit generates a zero-infrastructure public status page from a single YAML file committed to your repo: declare your endpoints, set check frequency, and it deploys a hosted status page, posts to Slack/Discord on downtime, and auto-resolves incidents when uptime returns. No dashboard to configure, no separate account to maintain — just a `healthpage.yml` in your repo and a URL you can give to customers.

## Mobile App Ideas (2)

**Idea 9: PubQuiz Coach 📱 MOBILE APP**
Regular pub quiz teams in the UK and Ireland take their hobby seriously — they track scores, know exactly which topics they lose points on every week, and spend the days before quiz night wishing they'd brushed up on 1980s pop music. PubQuiz Coach is a team quiz tracker: log your scores per round per category after each session, see your weak spots over time (always losing sports and geography, always winning music), and get flashcard-style warm-up rounds in your weak subjects before the next quiz. **Low competition:** the pub quiz as a serious competitive hobby has tens of millions of participants across the British Isles and zero purpose-built apps — the entire ecosystem lives on paper scoresheets. **Viral angle:** "Our team discovered we've never got a single history question right in four years" is exactly the kind of revelation people screenshot and post.

**Idea 10: FlatHunter Bingo 📱 MOBILE APP**
Anyone who's viewed a dozen apartments in a competitive rental market knows the experience: you leave each viewing unable to remember if the damp patch was in the second or fourth flat, whether the landlord of viewing three was the weird one, or why you rated two of them equally. FlatHunter Bingo is a viewing companion app: tap-off a checklist of red and green flags during each viewing (damp, storage, natural light, noise, landlord vibe, boiler age), auto-generate a scored "viewing report" you can share with a partner, and compare all your viewings side-by-side at decision time. **Low competition:** property portals are listing tools, not viewing companions — nobody has solved the "I can't remember which flat had the mould" problem. **Funny/viral angle:** the Bingo format for red flags ("Got a full house at viewing 7: damp, no storage, weird landlord, AND they called it 'cosy'") makes for instantly shareable flat-hunting horror stories.
