## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: FuelSplit**
Couples and families argue over who paid for gas last. FuelSplit is a simple mobile app that tracks shared vehicle fuel costs, splits them by usage/trips, and sends friendly nudges when someone owes. No spreadsheets, no awkward conversations.

**Idea 2: PhoneDetox Timer**
People know they're addicted to their phone but app blockers feel punishing. PhoneDetox is a "put-it-face-down" timer that rewards streaks of real offline time with visual progress — designed as a daily ritual, not a blocker. Think Duolingo-style gamification for digital wellbeing.

**Idea 3: NeighborTool**
Need a drill for 20 minutes? A ladder for one afternoon? NeighborTool is a hyper-local tool-sharing app where neighbors list what they own and borrow from each other — no fees, just trust scores. Saves money and reduces the "buy it once, use it once" waste cycle.

**Idea 4: MedPriceCheck**
Prescription prices vary wildly between pharmacies in the same city and nobody knows it. MedPriceCheck lets users scan or search their prescription and instantly see the cheapest nearby pharmacy. Particularly powerful in markets with unregulated pharmacy pricing.

**Idea 5: QuietHours**
Remote workers struggle to communicate their availability to family/housemates — sticky notes and closed doors don't scale. QuietHours is a minimal app that displays a glanceable focus status on a shared screen or phone widget, with a tap-to-interrupt request feature so family members can ask before barging in.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: FeatureDrop 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie hackers ship features but have no good way to announce them to existing users without blasting an email or posting on social. FeatureDrop is a lightweight in-app feature announcement widget (one JS snippet) with a hosted changelog page, email digest on new drops, and emoji reactions — so users actually notice and celebrate what you shipped.

**Idea 7: ReviewBridge 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Mobile devs know happy users rarely leave App Store / Play Store reviews on their own. ReviewBridge is an SDK that detects positive in-app moments (completed task, repeated usage, high session time) and triggers a frictionless review prompt with smart timing — plus a dashboard showing review velocity, rating trends, and A/B tested prompt copy.

**Idea 8: StagingURL 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Sharing a staging environment with clients or investors means exposing an ugly internal URL, dealing with basic auth, or setting up custom domains per project. StagingURL gives developers a shareable, password-protected public tunnel to any localhost port — with a branded short URL, optional expiry, and visit analytics. One CLI command, no infra.
