## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: TalentLedger**
Managers scramble at performance review time because impressive (and problematic) moments happened months ago and there's no record. TalentLedger is a private manager's log: jot down noteworthy moments per direct report throughout the year, tag by category (ownership, output, attitude), and export a structured summary when review season hits. Not HR software — a personal captain's log that turns "I think she did something great in March" into documented evidence.

**Idea 2: TradeAlert**
Collectors hunting for specific trading cards, vintage toys, sneakers, or vinyl know their targets but have to manually refresh Vinted, eBay, Facebook Marketplace, and Wallapop simultaneously — and scalpers always get there first. TradeAlert is a multi-platform saved-search monitor that fires a push notification the moment a matching listing appears, with price filters, condition filters, and a "watchlist" of ongoing targets. Single job, done well: be faster than the resellers.

**Idea 3: PermitDash**
DIY homeowners tackling renovations get lost in the permit process — what's required, what they've applied for, what stage it's in, which inspections are pending — and usually track it via a sticky note on the fridge. PermitDash is a project-specific permit diary: log your project type and municipality, track each open permit's status, attach uploaded documents, and get reminders for required inspections before deadlines slip. Not a permit-filing service — just the personal organizer the process desperately lacks.

**Idea 4: ChoreCredit**
Roommates fight about chores because contribution is invisible — one person does everything and nobody knows, or everyone thinks they're the one pulling the weight. ChoreCredit is a shared house fairness ledger: log what you cleaned or fixed (optional photo proof), see a running fairness score per housemate, and let the app handle rotating assignments without negotiation. Solves the passive-aggressive WhatsApp message at the root.

**Idea 5: ScentLog**
Perfume and fragrance collecting has exploded (YouTube, TikTok, Fragrantica communities) but serious collectors with 30–150 bottles have no dedicated catalog app — they use spreadsheets. ScentLog lets you log your collection, track wear frequency by season, record which notes you loved or didn't, build a wishlist, and get "what to wear today" suggestions based on weather and occasion. Small but obsessive audience that will pay $3.99 without hesitation.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: IntegrationSentry 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
SaaS apps silently break when a user's connected OAuth token expires or a third-party permission changes — neither the dev nor the user knows until the feature stops working and a frustrated support email arrives. IntegrationSentry is a background health monitor for your app's OAuth integrations: it pings each user's connected accounts on a schedule, detects expired or revoked tokens, triggers an in-app warning to the affected user, and fires a Slack/email alert to the dev team. Turns "this broke 3 weeks ago and we had no idea" into a proactive fix before it becomes churn.

**Idea 7: IndexNowDash 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers publishing SEO content — blogs, changelog pages, docs, new landing pages — have no visibility into whether Google or Bing have crawled and indexed their new URLs, or how long it's taking. IndexNowDash hooks into your deploy pipeline, automatically submits new and updated URLs to Google Search Console, Bing IndexNow, and other discovery endpoints on each deployment, then tracks indexing status per URL in a clean dashboard. The missing layer between "I published it" and "it actually ranks" — setup takes 10 minutes, insight is immediate.

**Idea 8: AppReviewCommand 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Mobile developers manage App Store and Google Play reviews in two separate dashboards with no cross-platform search, response tracking, or pattern analysis — responding to reviews is a context-switching chore that most indie devs just skip. AppReviewCommand is a unified review command center: all reviews in one feed, AI-drafted replies in your product's existing tone, a response-rate tracker (response rate directly affects store ranking), and keyword clustering that surfaces your top 5 recurring complaints at a glance. Turns 30 minutes of scattered clicking into a 10-minute daily ritual.

## Mobile App Ideas (2)

**Idea 9: GymSend 📱 MOBILE APP**
Bouldering and rock climbing gyms set new routes ("problems") every week, but members have no app for tracking which ones they've attempted, completed, or want to try — most use a notes app or just try to remember. GymSend lets you log attempts per route, rate the problem, set a goal ("I want to send the purple overhang"), and see what your gym friends have "sent" this week. **Low competition:** bouldering is growing explosively but app stores have zero purpose-built gym wall trackers (outdoor climbing apps exist, gym-specific ones don't). **Viral angle:** the "bragging rights" social layer — telling your climbing partner you sent something before them is half the fun.

**Idea 10: LARPio 📱 MOBILE APP**
Live action roleplay (LARP) has millions of participants globally but zero dedicated app — events are announced on Facebook, characters are managed in Word docs, costumes are tracked in photo albums, and the community has no home base. LARPio combines event discovery (by system: fantasy, sci-fi, Victorian, etc.), a character portfolio (stats, backstory, gear photos), and a post-event journal to log what happened. **Low competition:** nobody builds for LARPers because it sounds niche and ridiculous, which is exactly why it's wide open. **Funny/viral potential:** the contrast between how seriously LARPers take their hobby and the absurdity of the concept makes it instantly shareable content — every creator who covers it gets a viral moment.
