## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: WardrobeLoop**
Most people rewear 20% of their wardrobe while the rest collects dust. WardrobeLoop lets users photograph and catalog their clothes, tracks what they've worn, and suggests outfit combinations from what they already own — reducing "I have nothing to wear" syndrome and impulse buying.

**Idea 2: HomePulse**
Homeowners constantly forget when they last serviced their boiler, changed air filters, or cleaned gutters — until something breaks. HomePulse is a simple home maintenance tracker that logs every repair and service, sends seasonal reminders, and builds a service history you can hand over when selling.

**Idea 3: CoParentSync**
Divorced and separated parents juggle school events, medical appointments, and custody schedules across hostile communication channels. CoParentSync is a neutral shared calendar and expense tracker specifically designed for co-parenting — no personal messages, no drama, just coordination.

**Idea 4: UsedFair**
Selling or buying second-hand items on Facebook Marketplace or Craigslist is a guessing game — is €50 a fair price for this blender? UsedFair lets you snap a photo of any item and get an instant fair-market price based on recent listings across major resale platforms, so you never overpay or undersell.

**Idea 5: RentLog**
Renters often struggle to document noise complaints, maintenance requests, or landlord issues in a way that holds up legally. RentLog provides a timestamped, geotagged log of all communications and incidents — generating formatted PDF reports for disputes, deposit claims, or small claims court.

**Idea 7: ChurnNote 🛠️ DEV TOOL**

ChurnNote triggers smart exit surveys when users cancel or go inactive, uses AI to cluster responses into actionable themes, and sends a weekly digest highlighting the top reasons you're losing customers so you can actually fix them.
