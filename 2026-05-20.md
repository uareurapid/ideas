## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: RoadsideKit**
When your car breaks down or you're in a minor accident, your brain goes blank — most people have no idea what order to do things, what photos to take, what to say to the other driver, or who to call first. RoadsideKit is a step-by-step incident guide for car emergencies: accident workflow, breakdown checklist, roadside assistance numbers by country, and a structured photo-logging tool that captures what insurers actually need (wide shot, close-up damage, license plates, scene). The kind of app you download once and hope you never need — but can't leave home without.

**Idea 2: SiblingSplit**
When adult siblings share the cost of elder care — dad's private physio, mum's new stairlift, shared grocery deliveries, the cleaner who comes twice a week — the tracking is a WhatsApp thread and a lot of awkward conversations about fairness. SiblingSplit is a shared expense ledger specifically for sibling groups managing a parent's care: log contributions, set a recurring split formula, see the running balance per person, and generate a clear summary anyone can view. Same problem as flatmate bill splitting, but with a tone and design that respects the family context.

**Idea 3: ImmigrantAdmin**
Expats and immigrants have a pile of recurring bureaucratic deadlines that are completely opaque until you miss one — renew residence permit, update tax registration, file annual returns, exchange your driving licence, register with the health system — and the requirements differ by your country of origin × host country combination. ImmigrantAdmin is a country-pair task manager: pick where you're from and where you live, get a pre-built calendar of recurring obligations with document checklists and official links, and get reminders 60/30/7 days before each deadline. The "new in country, drowning in paperwork" app nobody has built properly yet.

**Idea 4: ConcertPal**
You find out your favourite band toured your city two months ago and sold out in hours — because you only follow them on Spotify and had no alert system. ConcertPal connects to your Spotify or Last.fm listening history, pulls in tour date announcements, and sends you early alerts for artists you actually listen to near your city. The twist: it has a lightweight group layer — share the alert with friends, see who's in, split ticket purchasing responsibility — so the "should we go?" WhatsApp thread starts with a clean signal, not a random screenshot.

**Idea 5: CourseDebt**
Most people own 20+ online courses across Udemy, Coursera, Skillshare, and YouTube playlists they've never touched, with no way to surface "what did I pay for that I still haven't finished?" CourseDebt is a personal course accountability tracker: manually log any course from any platform, set a finish-by date, mark progress in percentage, and get a weekly nudge with your current "debt." Not tied to any platform API — just your honest ledger of learning intentions vs. reality, with a gentle completion rate score that shames you into finishing what you started.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: MonorepoMap 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
In large monorepos, understanding which packages depend on what is a full detective job — developers regularly break things by changing a shared utility without knowing 6 downstream apps consume it, and tools like Nx or Turborepo require heavy config before they surface this. MonorepoMap scans your monorepo structure (Nx, Turborepo, pnpm workspaces, Yarn workspaces), generates a live visual dependency graph with one command, highlights circular dependencies, and lets you click any package to see its exact dependency tree and impact surface. The critical feature: a "what breaks if I change this?" simulation before you touch anything.

**Idea 7: BugReproKit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
The most expensive part of fixing a bug isn't writing the fix — it's spending 45 minutes trying to reproduce it from a vague user report like "it broke when I clicked submit." BugReproKit is a lightweight frontend SDK (one script tag or npm install) that records the exact DOM event sequence, network requests, console output, and relevant state snapshots in the 60 seconds before a crash, then packages it into a shareable, locally-replayable bug report with a one-click "reproduce this" link devs can run in their own browser. Like a black box flight recorder for web apps — the crash report becomes a reproducible test case, not a mystery.

**Idea 8: PricingSignal 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie founders almost universally underprice their SaaS — not because they don't want to charge more, but because they have no signal on what the market actually supports and competitors don't broadcast when they raise prices. PricingSignal monitors your specified competitors' pricing pages weekly for changes (detects plan structure shifts, price point changes, feature tier moves), scrapes public pricing discussions on Reddit/HN/Twitter for sentiment around your category, and delivers a monthly digest: "2 competitors raised prices in April, your $29/mo plan is below the market median for your feature set." Turns pricing from a gut-feel annual panic into a continuous data-informed process.
