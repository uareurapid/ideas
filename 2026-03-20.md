Based on my research through SearXNG, here are 5 new business ideas:
Idea 1: BrainBrick - ADHD Task Visualizer
People with ADHD struggle to see their progress because completed tasks "disappear into the void." A mobile app that turns each finished task into a visual brick, building a satisfying 3D structure over time. Gamifies productivity with tangible progress visualization, addressing the dopamine-seeking behavior common in ADHD brains.

Idea 2: ConvertBridge - CAD File Format Translator
Small manufacturers and design agencies waste hours manually converting files between different CAD software (SolidWorks, AutoCAD, Fusion360, etc.). A simple SaaS that auto-converts parametric models between formats with version tracking. One-click conversion for $15/month beats hiring a freelancer on Upwork every time.

Idea 3: ShopScheduler AI - Production Planner for Small Manufacturers
Job shops with 10-50 employees struggle with expensive, complex legacy scheduling tools. An AI-powered production scheduler that's simple to set up and actually understands small-batch manufacturing. Industrial businesses pay $300+/month without hesitation when you solve real pain.

Idea 4: ReviewRadar - G2/Capterra Alert System
Companies lose customers because they don't respond to negative reviews quickly enough on G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot. A monitoring tool that alerts teams via Slack/email the moment a new review drops, provides AI-suggested responses, and tracks response times. Helps prevent reputation damage before it spreads.

Idea 5: LaunchMetrics - Post-Product Hunt Reality Check
Startups confuse launch hype with product-market fit and waste months chasing vanity metrics. A dashboard that tracks what actually matters post-launch: Day 7/30 retention, conversion from free to paid, and Tuesday metrics (not launch day). Helps founders distinguish between launch energy and sustainable growth.
