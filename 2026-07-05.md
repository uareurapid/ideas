## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: ClosedLoop**
A platform connecting restaurants and cafes with near-expiry prepared food to community fridges and food banks in real time. Staff snap a photo of soon-to-be-tossed items and the app pings nearby receivers who can pick it up within a time window. Reduces food waste while giving businesses a tax-deductible donation trail automatically.

**Idea 2: TipFair**
Transparent tip pool calculator for hospitality workers. Servers scan their shift receipts with OCR, the app calculates fair tip-outs to bussers, bartenders, and kitchen based on actual sales data — eliminating the "manager did the math wrong" trust gap that causes constant drama in every restaurant. Export directly to payroll.

**Idea 3: FormBridge**
AI that takes any PDF government form (tax, visa, benefits, permits) and extracts exactly what's needed from a personal data vault. Upload your documents once (passport, pay stubs, lease), and FormBridge auto-fills any future form. For immigrants, freelancers, and anyone who's spent 3 hours deciphering box 14(b)(ii) on a government form.

**Idea 4: ShelfWatch**
Pantry management via barcode scanning. Once you open a product, ShelfWatch calculates the *actual* remaining shelf life — most items expire days or weeks after opening regardless of the printed date. Tracks opened-on dates and sends "use this soon" nudges. Different from recipe/freezer apps because it focuses purely on the opened-vs-printed gap nobody addresses.

**Idea 5: RoofSnap**
Homeowners take drone or phone photos of their roof after storms. AI analyzes for damage patterns (missing shingles, hail dings, lifted flashing) and generates a preliminary assessment with estimated repair ranges. Gives homeowners leverage before roofers show up — the industry has a reputation for inflating storm damage quotes.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: DemoLabs 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
SaaS sales tool that auto-generates demo-ready environments with realistic, industry-specific fake data from a natural language prompt. Sales engineers spend hours building convincing demos; this spins up sandboxes pre-loaded with data matching the prospect's industry. "Show me what this CRM looks like for a 50-person insurance brokerage." Saves 3-5 hours per sales demo.

**Idea 7: OutreachIntel 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Aggregates a prospect's recent digital footprint — GitHub commits, blog posts, HN comments, tweets, Product Hunt launches, conference talks — into a one-page briefing with personalized conversation starters. Cold outreach that references their actual recent work instead of "I saw you're the CEO at..." Dev founders selling to other devs need this desperately.

**Idea 8: RunbookSnap 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Watches your team's incident response in Slack/Teams and auto-generates postmortem runbook documentation from what actually happened. No more "we forgot to document the fix because everything was on fire." Captures the real sequence of diagnostic commands, who did what, and the actual resolution — then formats it into a clean runbook for next time.

## Mobile App Ideas (2)

**Idea 9: IsItCake? 📱 MOBILE APP**
AR camera app that guesses whether an object is secretly cake — riding the eternal "everything is cake" meme. Point your camera at anything suspicious-looking (that handbag? that shoe?) and get a cake probability score with exaggerated confidence levels. Niche: meme culture, party games, content creators. Absurdly specific, zero direct competition, and built-in viral potential because every result is shareable content. Think "Hotdog Not Hotdog" but for cake.

**Idea 10: RaveGrave 📱 MOBILE APP**
Yelp for cemeteries and graveyards. Taphophiles (cemetery tourists — yes, it's a real thing) rate and review graveyards on beauty, historical significance, famous residents, stonework quality, and "atmosphere." Includes grave-finding maps for locating specific historical figures. Niche community but deeply passionate — cemetery photography groups on Facebook have hundreds of thousands of members. Low App Store competition; the few existing apps are glorified map pins with no social/review layer.
