## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: LoyaltyExpiry**
Airline miles, hotel points, and credit card rewards expire silently — costing people hundreds in value they never know they lost, noticed only when trying to redeem. An app where you manually log or connect your loyalty program balances and expiry dates, getting alerts 60 days before points expire with a specific "burn it now" action suggestion (book a hotel, transfer, convert to gift card). The one financial category every major wallet and finance app completely ignores.

**Idea 2: PermitPal**
Expats, immigrants, and digital nomads juggle visa renewals, residence permits, tax filing deadlines, and health card renewals across multiple countries — each with its own bureaucracy, staggered timing, and severe penalties for missing. A document vault and deadline tracker specifically for cross-border admin: store a scan of each permit, set custom lead-time reminders, and get a simple renewal checklist per document type. The administrative layer every immigrant knows is a recurring nightmare and no general reminder app handles cleanly.

**Idea 3: BatchCook**
Weekend meal preppers want to cook efficiently but spend 30 minutes figuring out which recipes share ingredients, what can be cooked simultaneously, and how many portions they'll actually end up with. BatchCook takes the recipes you select, finds optimal cooking order and overlapping ingredients, generates a unified shopping list, and gives you a sequenced 2–3 hour prep plan so nothing is waiting cold while something else roasts. Not a recipe discovery app — a workflow optimizer for the people who already know what they want to cook.

**Idea 4: VolunteerLog**
Volunteers accumulate hours across charities, school events, and community organizations that matter for CVs, scholarship applications, professional development records, and sometimes tax deductions — but have zero easy way to log and prove them across multiple orgs. A simple volunteer hours ledger with organization tagging, GPS check-in, hour logging, and one-click PDF export that generates official-looking proof of service. Solves a real bureaucratic gap that the organizations themselves can't fix and apps like LinkedIn don't track.

**Idea 5: AnnualReview**
Most people intend to reflect on their year — what they achieved, where they traveled, books read, habits maintained, money saved — but have no single private place to track these as they happen, so the year-end review never materializes. A personal "annual review" app that prompts you to log notable events and milestones throughout the year with zero friction, then generates a beautiful, shareable annual summary report when December arrives. Part running journal, part year-in-review generator — designed for the introspective habit most people want but never manage to maintain.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: VisualGuard 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie devs and small teams ship UI changes that break layouts on specific viewports or dark mode — and nobody catches it until a user reports a scrambled header two weeks later. VisualGuard connects to your staging deployment, auto-captures screenshots across specified breakpoints and color schemes after each deploy, diffs them pixel-by-pixel against the last approved baseline, and posts a review comment on your PR with a side-by-side comparison of any changed UI regions. Visual regression testing that takes 5 minutes to set up versus the days a custom Playwright visual diff suite requires.

**Idea 7: DevBlogPilot 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Content marketing is consistently the highest-ROI long-term acquisition channel for dev tools and SaaS — yet almost no indie founders write consistently because drafting technically credible posts is a multi-hour investment per piece. DevBlogPilot analyzes your product, recent changelog, and GitHub activity, then drafts technically honest blog posts (how-we-built-X, engineering decisions, product learnings) formatted and auto-posted to Dev.to, Hashnode, and your own blog on a cadence you set. Distinct from release announcement tools — this generates original long-form content, not just release copy.

**Idea 8: MarketplaceLister 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
SaaS products get discovered through integration marketplaces (Zapier, Make, HubSpot App Marketplace, Pipedream, monday.com Apps) — yet creating and maintaining consistent listing profiles across all of them is repetitive busywork that most founders do once badly and never touch again. MarketplaceLister manages your integration marketplace presence from a single dashboard: sync descriptions, update screenshots, track install counts per marketplace, and get notified when a listing needs resubmission after a policy change. The distribution channel most solo devs are listed on but never actively optimize.
