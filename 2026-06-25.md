## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: GroupDecide**
Friend groups waste 20+ minutes in deadlocked "what do you want to eat?" text chains. GroupDecide uses ranked-choice voting with a Tinder-swipe UI — everyone swipes on restaurant/movie/activity options, the algorithm finds the overlap, and the decision is made in 60 seconds. No more "idk you pick" purgatory.

**Idea 2: EmergencyCard**
Your lock screen shows nothing useful in an emergency. EmergencyCard puts your medical info, allergies, blood type, ICE contacts, and medications on a Live Activity widget — visible without unlocking your phone, translated into the local language automatically. First responders look for this information but can't access it; this puts it front and center.

**Idea 3: CableDecode**
Everyone has a drawer of mystery cables. Point your camera at any cable or charger, and CableDecode identifies the type, voltage/amperage specs, and whether that generic USB-C cable is actually safe for your MacBook. Also warns about counterfeit or unsafe chargers. Simple computer vision + crowd-sourced cable database.

**Idea 4: PublicWashroom**
Map and review app for public restrooms. Cleanliness ratings, accessibility info, diaper-changing stations, gender-neutral options, door codes, and whether you need to be a customer. Underserved need for delivery drivers, people with IBD/Crohn's, parents with young kids, travelers, and anyone who's ever been desperate in an unfamiliar neighborhood.

**Idea 5: CollegePrep**
Month-by-month checklist for families sending a kid to college: immunization deadlines, dorm shopping with price comparison, financial aid tracker, campus-specific packing tips crowd-sourced from past students. Existing solutions are either generic school supply lists or overly broad "life admin" apps — nothing specific to the high-school-to-college transition.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: DepGuard 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Monitors your NPM/PyPI/Cargo dependencies and alerts you when a package has been abandoned (no commits in 6+ months), has unresolved critical issues, or the sole maintainer is signaling burnout. Developers unknowingly build products on dying dependencies and pay for it months later with painful emergency migrations. One `npm install` audit that actually watches over time.

**Idea 7: SessionReplay 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Privacy-first, indie-priced session replay for web apps. See exactly where users get stuck, rage-click, or abandon your onboarding. Existing tools like FullStory and Hotjar cost $100+/mo and are bloated — indie devs need a lightweight $15-20/mo option that auto-masks sensitive fields and respects GDPR by default. The "affordable observability" gap for solo founders is huge.

**Idea 8: CrossPostHub 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie devs waste hours manually copy-pasting launch announcements, changelogs, and devlogs across 20+ platforms (Dev.to, Hashnode, Hacker News, Reddit, Indie Hackers, Product Hunt, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Discord communities). CrossPostHub formats for each platform's conventions (title casing, link rules, character limits) and posts with one click. Includes scheduling and analytics on which platforms drive the most traffic.

## Mobile App Ideas (2)

**Idea 9: BodegaCat 📱 MOBILE APP**
A crowd-sourced map and catalog of store/bodega cats worldwide. Users submit photos, cat names, personality ratings, and whether the cat accepts pets. Like eBird or iNaturalist but exclusively for shop cats. Niche: cat internet culture meets urban exploration. Low competition — nothing dedicated to store cats exists. Built-in virality: every bodega cat that gets documented becomes free local PR for the shop, and cat content is the internet's universal language.

**Idea 10: SetlistGuess 📱 MOBILE APP**
Before a concert, fans predict the setlist. During the show, you check off songs as they're played in real-time. Leaderboards by artist, tour, and venue. Niche: jam band fans (Phish, Dead & Co.), festival-goers, Swifties tracking surprise songs, K-pop stans. Low competition — setlist.fm exists but has no prediction/gamification layer. Spreads organically through fan subreddits and Discords before every major tour. The competitive music-nerd angle gives it sticky weekly active use tied to tour schedules.
