## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: ComplaintLetterAI**
Filing complaints with airlines, utilities, insurance companies, and ISPs is a dark art — most people give up or write something that gets ignored. An AI service that turns your frustrated bullet points into a professionally-worded complaint letter with the right legal triggers, regulatory references, and escalation paths that actually get refunds and compensations. Monetize per letter or subscription.

**Idea 2: LockerRoom**
Cities are full of unused lockers — gyms, coworking spaces, universities, transit stations. Meanwhile, people need short-term storage near those exact locations. An "Airbnb for lockers" connecting locker owners/hosts with people who need to stash bags for a few hours. Commuters store work bags near stations, tourists drop luggage near attractions, event-goers stash purchases. Take a cut of each booking.

**Idea 3: ReceiptWarranty**
Receipts fade, get lost, or scatter across 17 different inboxes — and warranty claims require proof of purchase. An app that lets you snap a receipt once, then automatically extracts warranty periods from product databases and alerts you 14 days before each warranty expires with pre-filled claim forms. Also tracks which credit cards offer extended warranty benefits and reminds you to use them.

**Idea 4: FuneralStream**
Funerals are increasingly hybrid (in-person + remote attendees), but the tech is always terrible — bad audio from a phone propped on a tripod, or a generic Zoom link with no privacy. A simple, dignified service purpose-built for funeral homes: professional-quality private live streaming with a virtual guestbook, recording archive, and family-only access controls. Not Zoom, not YouTube — built for the moment.

**Idea 5: NoiseCheck**
When apartment or house hunting, you discover the noise situation after signing the lease. A community-driven noise map where residents log real decibel readings and subjective scores (street noise, neighbor noise, construction, flight paths) for specific addresses. Valuable for renters, home buyers, and even real estate agents who want to be transparent. Monetize via premium reports and real estate partnerships.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: DevBrandPilot 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Every developer should have a personal brand, but building one is tedious. A tool that auto-generates a polished portfolio site from your GitHub profile, writes blog-post drafts from your merged PRs and shipped features, creates social posts from your commit history, and maintains a "build-in-public" timeline. **Idea 7: SupportBotForge 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
AI support bots are everywhere but they all sound generic and hallucinate constantly — because nobody trains them on real company data. A tool that ingests your past support tickets, Slack conversations, docs, and internal wikis to generate a fine-tuned knowledge base and system prompt that actually sounds like your company. Bonus: it detects common question clusters in your existing tickets so you know what to automate first.

**Idea 8: MicroSaaSRadar 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
It's impossible to manually track all the tiny SaaS products launching daily. A competitive intelligence tool that monitors new launches across Product Hunt, Indie Hackers, Hacker News, GitHub trending, and Twitter/X — then alerts you the moment something enters your niche. Shows their pricing, tech stack (where detectable), initial traction signals, and early user sentiment. Designed for solo founders who need to know when someone is building in their lane.

**Idea 9: PigeonGram 📱 MOBILE APP**

**Idea 10: FaceCheck 📱 MOBILE APP**
A camera app that detects when you're making an unintentionally hostile or unwelcoming facial expression and alerts you with a subtle haptic buzz. For the millions of people who've been told "you looked angry" when they were just thinking, or "are you okay?" when they were totally fine. Uses on-device ML (Apple Vision/ML Kit), completely private — no images ever leave the device. Funny, deeply relatable, zero competition in the "involuntary facial expression alert" space, and the social media potential is enormous.
