## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: ClothCare**
People ruin expensive clothes because garment care labels are cryptic symbols nobody learns and "just throw it in with everything" is the default. An app where you photograph any care label and instantly get plain-English washing, drying, and ironing instructions — plus a gentle warning for delicate or dry-clean-only items you've logged. Tracks your wardrobe's care profile so you can confidently sort a mixed load without Googling every symbol.

**Idea 2: ClassGift**
Every school term, a class parent rep faces the same chaos: a group chat with 30 parents, half the money never collected, someone bought the wrong thing. ClassGift coordinates teacher and class group gifts — propose ideas, vote, set a target, collect payments with a link, and auto-close when funded. The PTA WhatsApp thread's most dreaded ritual, solved in a purpose-built tool.

**Idea 3: AirDiary**
People who buy indoor air quality monitors (Aranet, Airthings, Govee) get raw readings but no insight into why CO₂ spiked Tuesday or why allergy symptoms correlate with certain rooms. AirDiary lets you manually or automatically log readings from any sensor, tag events (cleaning, cooking, new furniture, windows open), and surface patterns over time with a weekly health correlation summary. The missing intelligence layer that sensor manufacturers never build.

**Idea 4: SizeKey**
Secondhand shopping on Vinted, Depop, and eBay is ruined by inconsistent sizing — a "Medium" from 2004 Zara fits nothing like a 2024 one, and vintage brands are a completely different universe. Enter your measurements and fit preferences; SizeKey tells you what size to search for in specific brands and eras, flagging listings that match your likely fit. Fewer failed purchases, fewer returns, more confident thrifting.

**Idea 5: HOALog**
Residents of HOAs and condo buildings often face maintenance stonewalling, neighbor conflicts, or rule-violation disputes — but have no organized record of what was reported, when, and what response came back. HOALog is a private documentation app where residents log timestamped entries with photos, upload communications, and build a clean paper trail over months or years — the kind of organized evidence that changes the outcome of an HOA dispute or small claims case.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: ReleaseNarrative 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Most indie devs announce new features with a dull "v2.3 is live — here's what changed" post that nobody reads. ReleaseNarrative takes your git commits, changelog, or bullet-point list and generates punchy, story-driven release announcements formatted for X/Twitter threads, LinkedIn posts, and newsletter updates — written like a human excited about the product, not a robot summarizing a diff. Turns the thing most founders procrastinate into a 30-second task.

**Idea 7: CustomerVoiceKit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Most indie SaaS founders have no idea why users churn — they just disappear, and founders are left guessing. CustomerVoiceKit embeds lightweight exit surveys (cancellation + inactivity), in-app NPS prompts, and a free-text feedback widget with a single script tag — then auto-summarizes recurring themes weekly ("5 users this week mentioned the CSV export is broken") delivered to Slack or email. Not just data collection: synthesized insight without a data analyst.

**Idea 8: PreLaunchSEO 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
The majority of indie devs launch with zero SEO foundation — no indexed content, no structured data, no keyword presence — and then spend months trying to build organic traction from ground zero. PreLaunchSEO guides you through a 2-week pre-launch SEO checklist: cornerstone page copy with keyword targeting, structured data markup, a blog content calendar with 10 seed articles to commission or write, and technical meta setup. Start launch day with a running start, not a blank slate.
