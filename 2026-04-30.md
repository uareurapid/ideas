## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: ReturnReminder**
Online shoppers constantly miss return windows — that item you weren't sure about sits in a closet until the 30-day deadline quietly passes. An app that detects purchases via email receipt scanning, logs each retailer's return deadline, and sends a push notification 3–5 days before it closes with a one-tap link to that retailer's return portal. Zero friction, pure money reclaimed.

**Idea 2: BabyFirsts**
Parents desperately want to capture baby milestones but scatter them across WhatsApp, Notes, and memory — and then forget the exact date their baby first walked or said "mama." A milestone journal that prompts parents at key developmental stages, stores a photo or short video per moment, and generates a beautiful shareable or printable timeline as a keepsake. Focused purely on the story of growth — not the feeding/sleep logging that existing baby apps obsess over.

**Idea 3: LocalizedMenu**
Tourists and food-curious locals struggle with foreign-language menus and can't visualize unfamiliar dishes even after translating the name. Point-your-camera app that translates menu items, shows crowd-sourced dish photos from that same restaurant, flags allergens, and surfaces what regulars actually order most. Fills the gap between Google Translate (text only) and TripAdvisor (not real-time per dish).

**Idea 4: MoveScore**
Renters visit apartments at 11am on a sunny Tuesday and have no idea what the neighborhood is actually like at night, on weekends, or in winter. MoveScore aggregates noise reports, walkability, transit reliability by time-of-day, and community vibe ratings into a neighborhood profile with time and day-of-week filters. Like Walkscore but real, time-aware, and crowd-sourced rather than algorithmic.

**Idea 5: VinylTracker**
Record collectors have no great dedicated app to catalog their collection, track what they've lent to friends, log plays, or get alerts when favorite artists release new vinyl. VinylTracker is a purpose-built collection manager with Discogs import, lending tracker with return reminders, listening log, and a personalized new release feed — for the passionate vinyl community that's been growing for a decade with zero great software to show for it.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: DocDiff 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
When developers bump a package version, breaking changes buried in changelogs cause silent bugs or hours of "why did this break" debugging. DocDiff hooks into your CI/CD pipeline, detects dependency version bumps, parses actual changelogs and release notes, and highlights breaking changes and deprecated APIs that are relevant to your specific code usage — not the full noisy diff. Saves hours every sprint and prevents regressions before they hit production.

**Idea 7: SaaSPulse 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Solo founders drown in raw metrics but don't know which numbers actually matter at their current stage. SaaSPulse connects to Stripe and a web analytics provider, then surfaces a focused dashboard of 5–7 stage-appropriate KPIs with benchmarks from comparable products at similar MRR ranges — so founders stop optimizing vanity metrics and focus on what actually drives the next growth level. No BI tool complexity, no data warehouse required, just actionable clarity for one-person shops.

**Idea 8: PortfolioForge 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers struggle to write compelling case studies about their own work — GitHub profiles list tech stacks but fail to communicate business impact. PortfolioForge analyzes your GitHub repos, commit history, and project descriptions, then generates polished case study narratives in impact-focused language (not "built a REST API" but "cut load times by 60% for 10k daily users"), outputting a hosted portfolio page that actually lands freelance clients and job offers.
