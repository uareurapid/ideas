## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: FreezerFog**
People freeze food with good intentions and rediscover unlabeled bags of mystery meat six months later — resulting in waste, guilt, and the same cycle repeating. FreezerFog is a minimal app to log what you freeze with a date and quantity, get nudged before items hit their recommended shelf life, and see meal suggestions based on what's currently in there. Solves a tiny recurring problem every household has and no pantry tracker addresses specifically for frozen food.

**Idea 2: MentalLoad**
The invisible cognitive labor of running a household — scheduling dentist appointments, remembering school forms, tracking when the kid needs shoes — falls disproportionately on one partner, and there's no shared language to surface it. MentalLoad is a couples' app where both partners log and claim household cognitive tasks, with a "load balance" view that makes invisible work visible without requiring an argument to expose it. Fills the gap between couples who feel the imbalance and couples who finally have a shared tool to address it.

**Idea 3: NeighborBorrow**
The same drill, pressure washer, and extension ladder sit unused in 20 different garages on any given street — yet people still buy rarely-used tools because they don't know who nearby has one. NeighborBorrow is a hyper-local item lending registry where you list what you're willing to lend, browse nearby listings, and initiate a time-bound borrow with a simple request — no payment, just community reciprocity. Turns neighborhood infrastructure into a sharing layer that also gives people a reason to actually meet their neighbors.

**Idea 4: WaitTimeLog**
Patients have no objective way to compare how much of their time different clinics, pharmacies, or government services actually consume — you only find out after you've already shown up. WaitTimeLog lets you log your arrival and "seen" times for any service, building a personal history of which providers respect your time and surfacing crowdsourced averages to help you choose before you book next time. A tiny data habit with real decision-making payoff that no existing booking or review platform captures.

**Idea 5: ReadingPace**
You grab a book for a two-week holiday or a long train journey and have no idea whether you'll finish it in time — and no reading app bothers to tell you. ReadingPace tracks how many pages you read per session, calculates your personal reading pace per book, and shows you a simple projection: "At your current pace, you'll finish in 11 days" or "You need 42 pages/day to finish before your flight." A genuinely useful thing no reading tracker surfaces, in an app small enough to actually use.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: LicenseGuard 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Most commercial SaaS products unknowingly violate open-source licenses — GPL dependencies that require source code disclosure, copyleft packages bundled into closed builds, attribution requirements never fulfilled. LicenseGuard scans your `package.json`, `requirements.txt`, or `Gemfile`, flags every license compatibility conflict with its specific legal risk level, and generates the NOTICES/attribution file you're legally required to ship. The compliance check nearly every indie dev skips until a lawyer mentions it.

**Idea 7: SupportToTestimonial 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
After a genuinely glowing customer support conversation in Intercom or Help Scout, that positive sentiment disappears forever — never converted into a review or testimonial. SupportToTestimonial monitors your support threads for strong satisfaction signals, identifies the moment a customer is happiest, and sends them a one-click testimonial request with a pre-filled quote drafted from what they actually said in the conversation. Converts support wins into landing page social proof automatically, at precisely the right moment.

**Idea 8: BounceDecoder 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie SaaS founders know their transactional or onboarding emails have low open rates but have no idea whether it's spam filtering, DKIM misconfiguration, content triggers, or list hygiene causing the problem — it's all buried in per-provider dashboards. BounceDecoder connects to your email provider (Postmark, SendGrid, Mailgun), analyzes bounce codes, complaint rates, and delivery patterns across your sending history, and produces a prioritized fix list with exact settings to change and content patterns to avoid. An actual diagnosis, not more dashboards.
