## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: CarDataLock**
The EU now mandates driver monitoring cameras in all new cars sold, meaning millions of people are being recorded inside their vehicles with zero transparency. An app that scans your car's make/model and shows you exactly what data it collects (cabin audio, eye tracking, GPS, biometrics), where it's sent, who it's shared with, and how to opt out where legally possible. A privacy dashboard for your car — think "Terms of Service; Didn't Read" but for the 2-ton surveillance machine in your driveway.

**Idea 2: CareShift**
Millions of families cobble together informal care for aging parents through neighbors, friends, and part-time helpers — but coordination is a mess of WhatsApp threads, sticky notes, and "did anyone give Mom her evening meds?" texts. A lightweight app that combines shift scheduling, medication logging, doctor visit notes, expense splitting, and secure family communication across all caregivers. Existing tools like CaringBridge are for sharing updates, not managing logistics — this is Asana for the unpaid caregiving army.

**Idea 3: TinyLandlord**
The "accidental landlord" wave keeps growing — people who moved but kept their old place, remote workers with ADUs, couples who merged households. These folks manage 1-2 units and are completely underserved: all existing tools (Buildium, AppFolio) assume 50+ doors. A simple app with state-specific lease templates, automated rent collection, maintenance ticket tracking, tax-prep summaries for rental income, and tenant communication — built for people who didn't plan on becoming landlords.

**Idea 4: KidDeviceControl**
Kids now touch 5+ devices across iOS, Android, Switch, school Chromebooks, and family tablets — and every single one has its own parental control settings buried in different menus. Parents are exhausted trying to manage screen time, app permissions, content filters, purchase approvals, and location sharing across this fragmented mess. A unified family tech dashboard that talks to every platform's parental controls API and gives you one place to manage it all, with kid-specific profiles that follow them across devices.

**Idea 5: DinnerLottery**
Weekly meal planning is soul-crushing and groceries are expensive. An app where groups of 3-5 neighboring families each cook one large meal per week for the whole group, rotating who cooks what. Built-in dietary preference matching, automatic recipe scaling, cost splitting, and a scheduling system that handles "I can't cook Tuesday because Timmy has soccer." Dinner co-op logistics — reduces each household's cooking workload by 75% and grocery waste by sharing bulk ingredients. The housing crisis pushed more people into close communities; this makes those communities functional.

**Idea 7: APIProspect 🛠️ DEV TOOL**

Lead generation specifically for API companies where the prospect has literally announced their need in public. Way more targeted than cold emailing "Head of Engineering" on LinkedIn.

**Idea 8: TestFlake 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Flaky tests are the silent productivity killer that every engineering team just accepts. A CI-integrated tool that tracks pass/fail patterns across builds, automatically identifies flaky tests using statistical analysis, surfaces the likely root cause (race conditions, async timing, environment drift, test ordering), and quantifies the cost in developer-hours wasted investigating false failures. Makes the invisible problem visible — most teams don't even know they have a 15% flake rate until they measure it. Think of it as a linting tool for test reliability.
