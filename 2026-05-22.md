## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: NannyLog**
Parents hiring nannies or au pairs struggle to track daily activities, meals, naps, and milestones in a shared, organized way. NannyLog is a simple shared app where caregiver logs the day and parents see a clean timeline — replacing the scattershot WhatsApp photo dump with a real record. Great for custody situations too.

**Idea 2: BikeService**
Cyclists have no easy way to track component wear across multiple bikes — chain mileage, brake pad wear, tire age — leading to surprise failures and costly damage. BikeService lets you log rides and automatically tracks wear per component, pinging you when service is due. Works for road, MTB, and e-bikes.

**Idea 3: HangoverPlan**
People planning bachelor/bachelorette parties, reunions, or group trips waste hours coordinating who's available and negotiating dates across WhatsApp threads. HangoverPlan is a dead-simple group event planner focused on casual social gatherings — pick dates, collect RSVPs, vote on activities, split costs. No login friction for guests.

**Idea 4: PantryBudget**
Families trying to reduce grocery waste don't know what's already at home before they shop, so they overbuy and throw away food. PantryBudget tracks what's in your pantry and freezer, generates shopping lists based on what's actually missing, and flags items about to expire. Saves money, reduces waste.

**Idea 5: FleaFinder**
Flea markets, car boot sales, and local pop-up markets have no good discovery layer — they're promoted via Facebook Events or flyers and are hard to find. FleaFinder aggregates local flea market listings, lets vendors claim a spot to announce what they're selling, and alerts bargain hunters about upcoming events near them.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: AppStoreReady 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
App Store and Google Play rejections are a black box — developers get cryptic rejection emails and have to guess what guideline they violated. AppStoreReady scans your app's metadata, screenshots, description, and known review policies, flags likely rejection triggers before submission, and suggests fixes. Saves weeks of back-and-forth delay on launch day.

**Idea 7: WaitlistWarm 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers build waitlists but have no structured way to keep leads warm during the build phase — most waitlists go cold and half the list churns before launch. WaitlistWarm is an automated nurture sequence tool built specifically for pre-launch SaaS: drip teaser emails, behind-the-scenes build updates, and a progress bar so signups feel invested in launch day.

**Idea 8: RedditMentionMap 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers want to know which subreddits and threads are actively discussing the problem their product solves, but Reddit search is terrible and there's no good tool to map the conversation landscape. RedditMentionMap lets you enter your product category or keywords and returns a ranked map of subreddits by relevance, recent mention volume, and sentiment — so you know exactly where your potential users already are.
