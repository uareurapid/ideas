## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: EstateUnraveller**
When a parent or relative dies, the surviving family is handed an avalanche of tasks — close bank accounts, cancel subscriptions, notify pension providers, settle outstanding bills — with no guide, no order, and no way to know when it's actually done. EstateUnraveller is a guided post-death admin checklist: enter the country and basic estate details, get a prioritised action list with deadlines, track what's resolved and what's outstanding, and export a completion report for the estate solicitor. Nobody wants to think about it in advance, which is exactly why the person who does stumble upon it while drowning in paperwork will pay anything reasonable.

**Idea 2: VenueScout**
Anyone trying to book a venue for a 30-person birthday dinner, private event, or team offsite knows the drill: three hours of Googling, calls to restaurants that don't pick up, and no idea which venues actually accommodate private hires vs. which just say they do. VenueScout is a curated private-hire directory filtered by real constraints — minimum/maximum headcount, catering options (BYO vs. in-house), deposit requirements, weekend availability — with verified listings contributed by people who've actually booked. The gap between "restaurant has a function room" and "you can use it for your actual event."

**Idea 3: AutoDossier**
When buying a used car privately, buyers are completely dependent on the seller's honesty — most don't know how to check for hidden finance, outstanding recalls, previous accident flags, or whether the MOT history matches the claimed mileage trajectory. AutoDossier takes a VIN or registration plate and generates a consolidated pre-purchase summary: finance check, MOT history with mileage plot, open recalls by make/model, and a "risk flag" summary in plain English. Not a replacement for a physical inspection — the digital pre-screening step that filters out the bad buys before you waste a Saturday.

**Idea 4: ConferenceRecap**
Professionals who attend industry conferences return with a notebook full of session notes they never look at again, business cards they forget to follow up on, and a general sense of "I should do something with all that." ConferenceRecap is a post-event synthesis tool: log sessions attended, dump your notes, scan or photograph cards, and get a structured "what I learned + what I committed to + who I need to follow up with" summary — ready to share with your team or act on yourself within 48 hours while it's still relevant. Turns conference FOMO into actual follow-through.

**Idea 5: FixedPriceFinder**
Freelancers, consultants, and tradespeople constantly undercharge for recurring work because pricing by the hour means every engagement is a new negotiation, and "I'm not sure" almost always defaults to too low. FixedPriceFinder is a productised pricing builder: input your service type, typical scope, hours, and desired rate — get a suggested fixed-price packaging with three tier options (core/standard/premium), a scope boundary checklist for each tier, and the market positioning rationale behind the numbers. The tool that turns "uh, €75 an hour, I suppose" into a clean pricing page.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: TestSeedFactory 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Every developer writes database seed scripts for testing — then rewrites them six months later when the schema changes, or discovers the seed data doesn't actually reflect production-realistic volumes and edge cases, so tests that pass locally blow up on staging. TestSeedFactory is an AI-assisted seed data generator: describe your schema in plain text or paste your migration file, specify realistic constraints (30% of users should have no avatar, 5% should have bounced emails), and generate SQL or JSON seed files that mirror actual production distributions. Stops the "passes locally, fails on real data" class of bug permanently.

**Idea 7: DeployGate 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers and small teams often ship breaking changes to third-party consumers — webhooks, public APIs, embedded widgets — without realising it, because their CI only tests their own code and not what downstream consumers depend on. DeployGate is a pre-deploy contract checker for public-facing interfaces: define your API contracts or webhook schemas in a simple YAML, run a check before deploy that verifies no breaking changes have been introduced, and get a diff showing exactly what changed and who it would affect. The "you broke our integration" Slack message, eliminated before the PR merges.

**Idea 8: TestimonialPipeline 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie founders know customer testimonials are among the highest-converting elements on a landing page, but collecting them requires either manually emailing happy users, hoping someone leaves a review unprompted, or adding "please review us" friction at the worst moment in the user journey. TestimonialPipeline is a lightweight testimonial harvesting flow: embed one SDK call triggered by a positive NPS response or a user milestone (first export, first shared result, 30-day mark), send a 2-question async prompt, auto-format the response into a display-ready quote, and post it to a moderation queue. Goes from "I should collect more testimonials" to having 20 on the landing page within 60 days of install.

## Mobile App Ideas (2)

**Idea 9: WoodID 📱 MOBILE APP**
Woodworkers, furniture restorers, and DIYers constantly encounter unidentified timber — offcuts at the lumber yard, mystery hardwood at an estate sale, inherited furniture they want to match — but have no quick reference to identify species by grain pattern, colour, and end-grain texture. WoodID is a timber identification app using photo analysis: snap the end grain and face grain, get the likely species with confidence rating, key properties (hardness, workability, finish behaviour), and common use cases. **Low competition:** plant ID apps are everywhere; wood species ID doesn't exist at all in the app store, yet the woodworking hobbyist community is enormous and deeply tool-loyal. **Viral angle:** "Identified the mystery wood in my 1920s dresser as Brazilian rosewood — extremely illegal to sell now, this is fine" is the exact kind of discovery that gets posted in every furniture restoration group on the internet.

**Idea 10: SocialEnergy 📱 MOBILE APP**
Introverts and people with social anxiety, ADHD, or autism know that some social events cost massively more energy than others — a 1-on-1 coffee is fine, a loud birthday party with 40 acquaintances is three-day recovery territory — but there's no tool to actually map your personal social energy budget and plan accordingly. SocialEnergy is a social calendar layer where you rate events by predicted and actual drain, build a personal "energy cost" profile for different social contexts, and get a weekly balance view so you can make informed decisions about what to accept. **Low competition:** the mental health app space is flooded with generic anxiety tools and breathing exercises — a specifically social-energy-aware calendar for introverts/neurodivergent adults is genuinely untouched. **Viral/funny angle:** "My app confirmed I should have said no to that work drinks thing — the data was right, it cost me Tuesday entirely" is deeply relatable content for every introvert on the internet, and the absurd seriousness of tracking your social exhaustion as data is exactly what makes it shareable.
