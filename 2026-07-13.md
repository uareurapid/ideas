## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: DrivewaySpot**
People near stadiums, concert venues, and festivals desperately need parking, while homeowners have empty driveways sitting unused. A peer-to-peer marketplace where homeowners list their driveway/garage spots for event parking and attendees book them for half the price of official lots. Think Airbnb for event parking — homeowner makes easy cash, driver saves money and avoids the post-event gridlock.

**Idea 2: GroupGiftPot**
Organizing group gifts is painfully awkward — one person fronts the money, then has to chase 8 coworkers for $15 each via Venmo reminders. A shared pot where everyone contributes privately toward a gift target, and funds only release when the goal is met. No more "hey, just following up on that $15..." texts. For office gifts, teacher appreciation, wedding group funds, and birthday pools.

**Idea 3: ProofPic**
Every rental deposit dispute, insurance claim, and eBay sale boils down to "when was this photo actually taken?" A camera app that cryptographically timestamps photos with verifiable proof — not just EXIF data anyone can edit. Landlords can't claim pre-existing damage, buyers can't claim items arrived broken, and insurers can't dispute timelines. Simple enough for anyone to use, strong enough to hold up in disputes.

**Idea 4: CallScreen**
Google's Call Screen is great — if you own a Pixel. An AI phone screener that works on any carrier and any device, answering unknown calls, asking who's calling and why, then sending you a transcript so you only pick up important calls. The spam filter your phone should have shipped with. Freemium: 20 screened calls/month free, unlimited for $3/month.

**Idea 5: PetSOS**
When your dog eats something they shouldn't and you're staring at a $4,000 emergency vet bill, GoFundMe is too slow and too broad. A pet-specific emergency crowdfunding platform with a pre-vetted community of animal lovers who micro-donate ($5-$20) to verified cases. Lower friction than general crowdfunding, trust built through vet verification, and donors can follow the pet's recovery.

**Idea 6: WaitlistKit 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Every indie hacker launches with a cobbled-together stack: Typeform → Google Sheets → Mailchimp → manual referral tracking → spreadsheet math. One line of JS gives you a beautiful waitlist page with built-in referral links, real-time viral coefficient dashboard, milestone-triggered emails ("you're #50! share to jump 10 spots"), and launch-day signup predictions. Stop reinventing the waitlist wheel.

**Idea 7: BugBountyLite 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie hackers can't afford HackerOne, but they'd happily pay users $20 to find that one crash bug. Embed a "🐛 Report Bug → Get Paid" widget in your app, set bounties by severity tier ($5-$50), let users submit with screenshots, and auto-pay approved reports via Stripe. Turns your early users into a QA team without the overhead. Pay only when someone finds something real.

**Idea 8: DomainWatch 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Your competitor launched a new product, switched payment processors, and is testing a freemium tier — and their DNS records tell you before their blog does. Monitor competitor domains for new subdomains (products?), DNS changes (providers?), expiring domains (acquisition targets?), SSL cert shifts, and tech stack changes. Competitive intelligence that runs silently in the background and surfaces only what matters.

## Mobile App Ideas (2)

**Idea 9: DialectDecode 📱 MOBILE APP**
Point your phone at someone speaking a thick Scottish brogue, Louisiana Creole, or rural Yorkshire dialect and get real-time "translation" to standard English. Equally practical (understanding your Glaswegian father-in-law) and hilarious (the translations will inevitably be wrong in spectacular ways). Niche: travelers in the UK, expats, and anyone who's nodded along pretending to understand. No real competitor in this space — speech-to-text tools exist but none handle strong regional dialects well. The viral potential from people sharing mistranslations is enormous.

**Idea 10: DogOrDate 📱 MOBILE APP**
A Tinder-style swipe game where you guess: is this profile a dating app bio or a dog adoption listing? "Loves long walks, cuddling on the couch, and treats" — could be either. Gets genuinely difficult, endlessly shareable, and would spread through screenshots faster than you can say "who rescued who." Niche: dog lovers and dating app satire enjoyers — two of the internet's most share-happy demographics. Zero competitors because nobody has thought to build this yet.
