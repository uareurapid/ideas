**Idea 1: TherapyStack**
Solo therapists and small practices spend 3-4 hours per week on billing, session notes, and insurance paperwork—time taken away from patients. An all-in-one micro-SaaS combining HIPAA-compliant note templates (AI-assisted), automated insurance claim generation, and simplified billing with payment reminders—specifically designed for 1-5 person practices, not enterprise EMR bloat.

**Idea 2: ReviewOwn**
SaaS founders are frustrated paying G2/Trustpilot $300+/month just to reply to their own customers while losing control of their reputation. A self-hosted review platform that captures feedback post-support ticket, displays social proof widgets on your site, and integrates with existing tools (Help Scout, Intercom, Canny) to create a feedback loop you actually own—no vendor lock-in, no ransom pricing.

**Idea 3: SyncLocal**
Small e-commerce sellers on Etsy, Depop, or local marketplaces manually update inventory across 3-5 platforms, leading to overselling and wasted hours. A micro-SaaS offering one-way inventory sync from a master platform to underserved local/niche marketplaces (not Amazon/eBay—those are covered), with instant stock updates and order notifications—focusing on the long tail of marketplaces that big tools ignore.

**Idea 4: ValidateKit**
Indie hackers struggle with "false signals" when validating SaaS ideas—people say they'll pay but never convert. A validation toolkit combining Reddit/forum mention tracking, automated landing page A/B tests, and a "pay-to-join-waitlist" widget ($5-20 deposits that prove intent)—helping founders identify real demand before building, not just collecting vanity email signups.

**Idea 5: ProgressBrick**
Remote learners and side-project builders lose motivation because progress feels invisible and lonely. A mobile app where every completed task (coding session, workout, language lesson) adds a "brick" to your visual tower; follow friends' towers in topics you care about, see what they're building, and get accountability through shared progress—gamifying consistency without complex social features.
