## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: HobbyMaintenance**
Every serious hobby comes with equipment that needs periodic upkeep — restring a guitar every 3 months, service a bike annually, wax skis before the season, clean a camera sensor — but there's no single app to track this across hobbies, and people only remember to do it when something breaks. HobbyMaintenance is a hobby equipment log and service reminder app: add your gear, set maintenance intervals per item, log service history, and get timely nudges. Think "car maintenance app" but for all the expensive gear sitting in your garage or studio.

**Idea 2: TimezoneBuddy**
Remote workers and international freelancers constantly do time zone math — "if it's 3pm here, is it a good time to ping my client in Singapore or my contractor in New York?" — but world clock apps show cities, not people. TimezoneBuddy is a person-centric world clock: save your recurring contacts with their city, and instantly see one screen showing "right now, good time for: Ana, Rui, Jake" and "sleeping: Mei, Carlos." Shifts the mental model from geographic clocks to human availability at a glance.

**Idea 3: MeetingCost**
Professionals sit through bloated meetings with no visceral sense of how much collective time and money is being consumed in real time — all the productivity guilt is abstract. MeetingCost is a dead-simple mobile app: input the number of attendees and a rough average salary, hit Start, and watch a live dollar/euro counter tick up while your meeting runs. At the end it shows "this meeting cost approximately €1,840 in salaries" — not a management tool, just the psychological nudge that gets meetings ended sooner.

**Idea 4: SchoolSupplyRun**
At the start of every school year, parents receive a supply list and coordinate the shopping across two adults, two stores, and sometimes two or three kids with different lists — resulting in duplicate purchases, missed items, and the same WhatsApp thread chaos every September. SchoolSupplyRun lets you paste or photograph the school supply list, converts it to a live shared checklist that syncs across both parents' phones in real time, and carries the list forward year-to-year so you know what's already at home. Hyper-seasonal, hyper-specific, and nobody has solved it.

**Idea 5: BreweryPassport**
Craft beer enthusiasts visit taprooms and breweries but have no dedicated way to log which they've been to, what they drank, and build a shareable trail for friends planning a trip to the same city. BreweryPassport is a check-in app for brewery visits: log the beers you tried, rate the experience, build a personal map of visited spots, and share your "passport" with friends. Untappd tracks individual beers with a social feed; this is specifically about the brewery visit as the destination experience — closer to a travel passport than a beer rating app.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: ChangelogGrowth 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Most SaaS changelogs are dry developer release notes nobody reads — but a well-written changelog is actually a retention tool that keeps users engaged, reduces "is this product still alive?" churn, and converts changelog visitors into upsells. ChangelogGrowth pulls your GitHub releases, PR titles, and commit messages, rewrites them in benefit-focused user language (you approve/edit), and publishes to a beautiful hosted changelog page with email/RSS subscription and automatic Slack/Discord announcements. Distinct from DevBlogPilot (long-form blog posts) — this is specifically the changelog-as-product-marketing pipeline, shipped in under an hour.

**Idea 7: TestDataForge 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Developers building and testing SaaS features need realistic fake data — but Faker.js requires custom scripting per schema, production data can't be used for privacy reasons, and non-engineer teammates can't generate test datasets without dev help. TestDataForge is a managed test data service: paste your DB schema or connect via API, configure what "realistic" looks like for your domain (e-commerce orders, SaaS user accounts, healthcare records), and it generates and seeds coherent relational fake data into your dev or staging environment in one click. The key differentiator is a no-code UI so QA and product managers can spin up fresh test environments without a dev.

**Idea 8: AffiliatePilot 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie SaaS products leave significant revenue on the table by not running a refer-a-friend or affiliate program — but Rewardful, PartnerStack, and FirstPromoter all require days of integration work and monthly fees that don't make sense at sub-$5k MRR. AffiliatePilot is a lightweight affiliate layer for indie SaaS: connects to Stripe, Paddle, or Lemon Squeezy in 10 minutes via OAuth, auto-generates shareable referral links for any user, tracks conversion attribution, and handles automated payouts via PayPal or Stripe payouts. Specifically the "I want a working refer-a-friend program by tomorrow without writing webhook code" solution.
