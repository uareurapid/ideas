## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: SeniorGuide**
Adult children spend hours on phone calls trying to walk elderly parents through basic smartphone tasks — only for the parent to forget by next week and call again. SeniorGuide lets you create a personalized, visual step-by-step guide library for your parent's exact phone and apps ("How to video call on WhatsApp," "How to pay with MB Way") that lives on their screen as a simple big-button app, plus a one-tap "help me" button that initiates a screenshare session directly with you. Bridges the gap between "just Google it" and your parent's patience running out at step two.

**Idea 2: MovingOS**
When you move home, there are 50+ things to do — notify your bank, transfer electricity, cancel the internet, update your address with the tax authority, redirect mail, register kids at a new school — and people discover each one only when something breaks. MovingOS generates a personalized moving checklist based on your situation (renter/owner, kids, pets, country), tracks what's done vs. pending, pre-fills official notification forms where possible, and reminds you of time-sensitive items like utility cutoff deadlines. Different from MoveScore (neighborhood rating) — this is the operational logistics of the move itself.

**Idea 3: BasketCompare**
Supermarkets in the same city can differ by 20–40% on a standard weekly shop, but you only discover this after years of habit — no app lets you build your actual basket and see what it costs across the stores near you before you leave the house. BasketCompare lets you build a recurring grocery list, crowdsources prices per item per store updated weekly, and tells you "your basket is €38 at Lidl, €51 at Continente, and you could save €9 by splitting these 4 items." Hyper-practical, hyper-local, and no major grocery app surfaces this basket-level comparison.

**Idea 4: SoloCheckIn**
Solo travelers — especially women traveling alone — have no clean, low-friction way to keep trusted contacts informed if something goes wrong. SoloCheckIn lets you set a journey itinerary with scheduled "I'm safe" check-in timers: if you don't confirm within the window, it escalates — first a ping to you, then a message to your emergency contacts with your last known location and itinerary details. Minimal, calm UX for normal conditions; serious safety net if you don't check in.

**Idea 5: PetMeds**
Pets with chronic conditions — diabetic dogs needing twice-daily insulin, epileptic cats on phenobarbital, animals undergoing cancer treatment — require precise daily medication logging that owners currently track in notebooks, WhatsApp chats, or not at all. PetMeds is a minimal medication schedule and symptom diary for sick pets: log doses, note reactions, track seizure frequency or glucose readings, and generate a clean PDF timeline to bring to every vet visit. The clinical detail layer that PetChart and general pet apps don't go near.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: AppReviewReply 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers know they should reply to App Store and Google Play reviews — especially the 1–3 star ones that hurt conversion — but doing it consistently is low-priority work that never happens. AppReviewReply monitors your store reviews, drafts a personalized reply in your brand voice for each one (pulling in context from what the reviewer actually said), lets you approve with one tap or edit and post, and tracks whether a review rating later improved after your response. Not a review analytics dashboard — specifically the reply workflow that converts unhappy users into reconsidered ratings.

**Idea 7: PressKitOS 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
When a journalist, podcaster, or newsletter writer wants to cover your product at 11pm on a deadline, they need a press kit — and most indie founders have nothing ready, losing the coverage. PressKitOS auto-generates a beautiful, always-current press kit page from your app store listing, GitHub profile, and a short onboarding form: pulls screenshots, extracts key stats you choose to share, drafts a founder bio template, and maintains a "coverage tracker" where mentions automatically appear. One link to share with anyone who asks — no Notion doc to keep updated, no Figma to export from.

**Idea 8: UpgradeMap 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Framework and library major version upgrades (React 18→19, Next.js 14→15, Node 18→22, Rails 7→8) contain breaking changes that affect different codebases differently — yet migration guides assume a clean codebase and force developers to manually figure out which of 40 breaking changes actually apply to them. UpgradeMap scans your repo for a target version bump, identifies only the breaking changes relevant to your actual code, generates a personalized step-by-step migration plan with the exact files and lines that need touching, and lets you check off progress as you go. Turns the dreaded "we've been on v14 for 18 months" situation into a scoped, trackable project.
