## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: GriefAdmin**
When someone dies, the family is hit with 40+ administrative tasks while grief is at its peak — cancelling subscriptions, notifying pension funds, handling estate paperwork, writing thank-you notes, closing bank accounts — and each one is discovered reactively when a bill or letter arrives. GriefAdmin provides a guided, country-specific checklist for the administrative aftermath of bereavement: what to do, in what order, who to contact, and what documents to bring — with a gentle cadence that respects the emotional weight. Not a legal will tool, not a vault — just the to-do list people desperately need and no one has built with this sensitivity.

**Idea 2: SubLandlord**
Millions of people rent out a single spare room, sublet to a flatmate, or temporarily host someone — situations where full landlord software (Buildium, Landlord Vision) is total overkill, but a WhatsApp thread and a spreadsheet is genuinely inadequate. SubLandlord is a micro-scale rental manager: log rent payments, split utility bills by formula, document check-in condition with photos, store the deposit amount, and generate a simple PDF statement at the end of the tenancy. Designed specifically for the one-room-one-tenant scenario that affects millions but has zero purpose-built software.

**Idea 3: EventProgram**
Community event organizers — school concerts, amateur theatre groups, local 5K races, church festivals, graduation ceremonies — need a printed or digital program but have nothing between "Word doc nightmare" and paying a graphic designer. EventProgram is a template-driven event program builder: fill in the schedule, performers/participants, sponsors, and notes, pick a layout, and it generates a beautiful print-ready PDF or a sharable digital page you can QR-code at the door. Solves a recurring pain for thousands of volunteer organizers who currently spend an evening fighting with Publisher.

**Idea 4: SleepWindow**
Shift workers, new parents, and chronically inconsistent sleepers can't use standard sleep schedule apps because their "optimal bedtime" isn't fixed — it depends on when they actually slept the last 3–5 days. SleepWindow tracks your recent actual sleep times (manual entry or Health/Google Fit sync), runs a simple circadian model, and tells you "tonight your best sleep window is 11:15 PM – 7:00 AM — here's why" plus a smart alarm suggestion. Not a sleep quality app, not white noise — specifically the scheduling intelligence that helps irregular sleepers stop fighting their own rhythm.

**Idea 5: WalkReport**
Freelance dog walkers who manage several regular clients have no lightweight way to send a quick post-walk update — GPS confirmation, a photo, and a "peed twice, pooped once, pulled a lot on the lead" note — without signing up to expensive pet-care platform subscriptions. WalkReport is a $5/month solo walker app: tap to start a walk, log bathroom events, take one photo, tap to end, and it automatically sends a branded summary card via WhatsApp or SMS to the owner. Fills the exact gap between the "professional platform" tier and the chaos of manual messages.

## Developer Tools (3)

**Idea 6: OnboardWatcher 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Most indie SaaS founders have no idea where in their onboarding flow users are silently giving up — Mixpanel and Amplitude require a data analyst mindset and 2-day setup, so the typical indie dev ships blind. OnboardWatcher is a single script tag that auto-instruments key UI steps (form completions, button clicks, page progressions), builds a visual drop-off funnel in real-time, and sends a weekly plain-English Slack/email digest: "72% of users who reached step 3 never started step 4 — these are the last 8 who dropped." No data warehouse, no custom events, no configuration — just the activation funnel in under 5 minutes.

**Idea 7: VideoTestimonial 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Video testimonials convert 3–5x better than text quotes on landing pages, yet almost no indie SaaS has them — because asking users to "film a testimonial" feels like a huge ask and most founders never do it. VideoTestimonial sends an automated, friendly recording request triggered by a high NPS score or a milestone event, opens a dead-simple in-browser one-take recorder (no software install, no Loom account), and delivers the trimmed clip with auto-generated transcript straight to your dashboard. Takes the ask from "awkward favour" to a 45-second flow that 15–25% of happy customers complete without friction.

**Idea 8: SocialDMPipeline 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Cold email open rates in 2026 are crushed by spam filters — but LinkedIn DMs and Twitter/X DMs from a real account still get 30–50% read rates for dev tools and B2B SaaS, yet no indie founder has a system for it. SocialDMPipeline lets you define a target persona, discovers matching profiles via LinkedIn/X search, drafts a personalized first-touch message drawing context from each person's recent posts or bio, and manages the full pipeline (sent → replied → converted) across both platforms in one view. Turns the one acquisition channel that's still working in 2026 from a manual grind into a 30-min-per-week system.
