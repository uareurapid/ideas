## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: SpotDrop**
A marketplace for last-minute cancellation spots at fully booked local businesses. Restaurants, barbers, vets, dentists, therapists — when someone cancels, the slot goes live and nearby users get pinged. Businesses fill empty tables/chairs, users skip the 3-month waitlist for that hot new place.

**Idea 2: PatchPulse**
Hyperlocal neighborhood safety coordination done right. Verified residents share and track incidents, get real-time alerts scoped to their specific street or block, and coordinate with local law enforcement. No Nextdoor racism or Facebook drama — just actionable safety intel with identity verification and privacy controls.

**Idea 3: FormFriend**
AI that reads any government, insurance, or medical form, explains what each cryptic field actually means in plain language, and walks you through filling it out correctly. The "TurboTax for everything that isn't taxes" — visa applications, disability benefits, building permits, FAFSA, insurance claims. Millions of people abandon forms out of confusion every year.

**Idea 4: WattWise**
Home energy optimization built for the dynamic pricing era. Tracks appliance-level usage patterns, predicts the cheapest hours to run high-consumption devices based on your utility's real-time rates, and calculates genuine ROI for solar, battery, and heat-pump investments using your actual data — not generic estimates that assume you live in Arizona.

**Idea 5: DeadManSwitch**
Digital afterlife planning for the modern estate. You designate what happens to every account — email, social media, cloud storage, crypto wallets, subscription services — when you pass away. Executor gets a clean dashboard with step-by-step instructions. No more families locked out of six-figure Bitcoin wallets or battling Google for access to a deceased loved one's photos.

**Idea 8: APIAnchor 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Insurance for your third-party API dependencies. Continuously monitors every external API your app calls. When one announces deprecation, introduces breaking changes, hikes pricing, or experiences downtime, it auto-generates an impact report showing exactly which endpoints in your codebase are affected and suggests migration code snippets for alternatives. Prevents the "Stripe/Plaid/Twilio just broke our app and we found out from a Hacker News comment" panic.
