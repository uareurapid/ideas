## General Ideas (5)

**Idea 1: VanRoute**
Van lifers and road trippers have no dedicated route planner that accounts for campsite availability, boondocking spots, dump stations, water refills, and vehicle-specific constraints (height limits, weight limits on bridges). VanRoute is a community-sourced map app where dwellers plan routes around real rig logistics, with offline mode and reviews from fellow travelers. Taps into the massive and underserved van life / overlanding community that has outgrown generic map apps.

**Idea 2: SideHustleBooks**
Freelancers and gig workers are terrible at tracking income across platforms (Fiverr, Upwork, Airbnb, eBay) and panic every tax season. A mobile-first bookkeeping app that aggregates gig income from connected platforms, auto-categorizes expenses from receipt photos, estimates quarterly tax obligations in real time, and exports a tax-ready report. Strips out all the accountant-facing complexity of QuickBooks and Wave — built purely for the solo earner.

**Idea 3: GriefGuide**
When someone dies, the surviving family faces a mountain of practical tasks nobody prepares you for: canceling subscriptions, notifying banks, closing email accounts, dealing with the estate, contacting government agencies. A step-by-step app that walks you through all post-bereavement logistics with jurisdiction-specific checklists, letter templates, and deadline reminders — so grief isn't compounded by administrative overwhelm. (Distinct from pre-death planning apps — this is about what happens *after*.)

**Idea 4: CraftPricer**
Handmade sellers on Etsy and at craft markets consistently undercharge, failing to account for materials, labor, packaging, platform fees, and shipping. A mobile calculator where you log your materials cost, hours worked, and overhead — and it instantly outputs a recommended profitable price, your true margin, and a breakdown showing where your money goes. Targets the massive "I love making things but I'm losing money" problem in the creator economy.

**Idea 5: RoommateScore**
Finding a roommate online is risky — Marketplace and SpareRoom profiles tell you nothing real about living habits. RoommateScore lets people build a verified living profile (schedule, cleanliness standards, guest tolerance, noise levels), collect references from past roommates, and verify their identity — creating a portable "roommate reputation" that makes shared housing safer. Like Airbnb's review system, but purpose-built for co-living.

**Idea 6: OnboardFlow 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Most indie SaaS products churn users in the first 3 days because onboarding is an afterthought — users bounce before reaching their "aha moment." OnboardFlow adds contextual tooltips, task checklists, and completion progress to any web app with one script tag, then surfaces a funnel showing exactly which onboarding step kills activation. **Idea 7: PrivacyPage 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie developers routinely skip proper privacy policies, GDPR/CCPA compliance, cookie consent banners, and Terms of Service — risking App Store rejection, regulatory fines, or user trust damage. PrivacyPage generates legally sound, app-specific privacy policies and ToS from a 5-minute questionnaire, auto-updates clauses when regulations change, and provides a hosted page plus embeddable widget. Cheaper and faster than a lawyer, smarter and more defensible than generic free templates.

**Idea 8: APIMonitor 🛠️ DEV TOOL**
Indie SaaS products silently break when third-party APIs they depend on (Stripe, OpenAI, Twilio, SendGrid, etc.) degrade — and developers only find out when users start complaining. APIMonitor tracks the status pages and response times of every external API in your stack, correlates third-party degradation against your own error rate spikes, and sends you an alert before your inbox fills up with angry support tickets. Fundamentally different from uptime monitors — it watches *their* infrastructure, not yours.
